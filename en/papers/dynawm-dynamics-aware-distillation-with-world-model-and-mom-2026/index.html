Deep Analysis

Innovations

  • World model as a regularizer to enforce forward-dynamics awareness, preserving comprehensive terrain geometry and enabling hierarchical encoding visualization
  • Momentum target encoder to provide consistent distillation targets, preventing dimensional collapse from non-stationary teacher updates

Methodology

DynaWM is a dynamics-aware representation learning framework built on a teacher-student paradigm. It incorporates a world model regularizer to enforce forward-dynamics awareness and a momentum target encoder to stabilize knowledge transfer. Evaluation is conducted via PCA visualization and quantitative metrics on terrain encoding, with experiments in simulation and on real hardware.

Key Results

The method achieves superior terrain adaptability and motion smoothness, enabling bipedal-wheeled robots to traverse diverse continuous stairs in both simulation and real hardware experiments.
