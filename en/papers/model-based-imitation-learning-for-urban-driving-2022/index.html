Deep Analysis

Innovations

  • Jointly learns a model of the world and a policy for autonomous driving
  • Leverages 3D geometry as an inductive bias
  • Learns a highly compact latent space directly from high-resolution videos of expert demonstrations
  • Trained on an offline corpus of urban driving data without any online interaction
  • Predicts diverse and plausible states and actions interpretably decoded to bird's-eye view semantic segmentation
  • Executes complex driving manoeuvres from plans entirely predicted in imagination
  • First camera-only method that models static scene, dynamic scene, and ego-behaviour in urban driving

Methodology

MILE is a model-based imitation learning approach that jointly learns a world model and a driving policy. It uses 3D geometry as an inductive bias to learn a compact latent space from high-resolution expert demonstration videos, and is trained offline on urban driving data without any online interaction with the environment.

Key Results

MILE improves upon prior state-of-the-art by 31% in driving score on the CARLA simulator when deployed in a completely new town and new weather conditions.

Limitations

  • Only evaluated in simulation (CARLA simulator)
  • Requires an offline corpus of expert demonstrations
  • No online interaction with the environment during training may limit adaptation to unseen scenarios
