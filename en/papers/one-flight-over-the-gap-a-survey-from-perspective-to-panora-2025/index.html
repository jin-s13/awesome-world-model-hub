Deep Analysis

Innovations

  • Systematic categorization of panoramic vision into four major categories: visual quality enhancement and assessment, visual understanding, multimodal understanding, and visual generation.
  • Cross-method analysis of strategies addressing panoramic-specific challenges (geometric distortions, non-uniform sampling, periodic boundary continuity) across different tasks.
  • Cross-task comparison and classification of panoramic vision research, covering 20+ representative tasks from over 300 papers.

Methodology

The survey revisits the panoramic imaging pipeline and projection methods to establish prior knowledge, then summarizes three key challenges of perspective-to-panorama adaptation: severe geometric distortions near the poles, non-uniform sampling in Equirectangular Projection (ERP), and periodic boundary continuity. It covers 20+ representative tasks from over 300 research papers, presenting a cross-method analysis of strategies and a cross-task comparison, and classifies panoramic vision into four major categories.

Key Results

The survey identifies three core challenges for domain adaptation from perspective to panoramic vision and categorizes the field into four major areas: visual quality enhancement and assessment, visual understanding, multimodal understanding, and visual generation.

Limitations

  • The survey is a review of existing literature and does not propose new methods or experimental validation.
  • The coverage of 20+ representative tasks may not be exhaustive of all panoramic vision research.
  • Open challenges and future directions in data, models, and applications are discussed but not resolved.
