Model as a Game: On Numerical and Spatial Consistency for Generative Games

arXiv 25.3 2025 32.2 method

TLDR

Proposes numerical and spatial consistency modules for generative games, improving consistency with minimal overhead.

Reasoning

The paper addresses a key limitation in generative games—maintaining numerical and spatial consistency—and introduces novel modules based on DiT architecture. Strengths include clear problem definition and empirical validation across three games. Weaknesses are the narrow focus on 2D games and lack of discussion on scalability or broader applicability.

Deep Analysis

Innovations

  • Revisiting the paradigm of generative games as 'Model as a Game' (MaaG) with a well-developed mechanism
  • Empirical study on the 'Traveler' 2D game created by an LLM to highlight consistency challenges
  • Numerical module integrating a LogicNet to determine event triggers with external calculations as conditions for image generation
  • Spatial module maintaining a map of explored areas, retrieving location-specific information during generation, and linking new observations for continuity
  • Integration of both modules into the DiT architecture to achieve numerical and spatial consistency
  • Demonstration of significant consistency improvements with minimal time overhead across three games

Methodology

The paper builds on the DiT architecture and introduces two specialized modules: a numerical module that uses a LogicNet to determine event triggers, with calculations processed externally as conditions for image generation; and a spatial module that maintains a map of explored areas, retrieving location-specific information during generation and linking new observations to ensure continuity. Experiments are conducted across three games, comparing against baselines on consistency metrics.

Key Results

The integrated modules significantly enhance performance on numerical and spatial consistency metrics compared to baselines, while incurring minimal time overhead during inference.
