Deep Analysis

Innovations

  • Hierarchical modeling of agents' behavior by inferring persistent latent traits (e.g., skills, preferences) from interaction trajectories
  • Trait-based social world model that predicts agents' responses to deployed mechanisms
  • Online trait inference during real-world interactions to boost policy learning efficiency

Methodology

SWM-AP learns a social world model that hierarchically infers agents' latent traits from their interaction trajectories and uses a trait-based model to predict agents' responses to mechanisms. The mechanism design policy collects extensive training trajectories by interacting with the social world model, while concurrently inferring agents' traits online during real-world interactions to further enhance sample efficiency.

Key Results

In experiments on tax policy design, team coordination, and facility location, SWM-AP outperforms established model-based and model-free RL baselines in both cumulative rewards and sample efficiency.
