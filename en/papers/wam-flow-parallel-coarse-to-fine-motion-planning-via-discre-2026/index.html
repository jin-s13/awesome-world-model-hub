Deep Analysis

Innovations

  • Casting ego-trajectory planning as discrete flow matching over a structured token space
  • Fully parallel, bidirectional denoising enabling coarse-to-fine refinement with tunable compute-accuracy trade-off
  • Metric-aligned numerical tokenizer preserving scalar geometry via triplet-margin learning
  • Geometry-aware flow objective
  • Simulator-guided GRPO alignment integrating safety, ego progress, and comfort rewards while retaining parallel generation
  • Multi-stage adaptation converting a pre-trained autoregressive backbone (Janus-1.5B) to a non-causal flow model with continued multimodal pretraining

Methodology

WAM-Flow is a vision-language-action model that performs parallel coarse-to-fine trajectory planning via discrete flow matching. It uses a metric-aligned numerical tokenizer with triplet-margin learning, a geometry-aware flow objective, and simulator-guided GRPO alignment for safety and comfort. The model is adapted from a pre-trained autoregressive backbone (Janus-1.5B) through multi-stage conversion and continued multimodal pretraining. Evaluation is conducted on the NAVSIM v1 benchmark using PDMS metric, comparing against autoregressive and diffusion-based VLA baselines.

Key Results

1-step inference achieves 89.1 PDMS and 5-step inference reaches 90.3 PDMS on NAVSIM v1 benchmark, outperforming autoregressive and diffusion-based VLA baselines.
